Behavior-Aware Probabilistic Routing For Wireless Body Area Sensor Networks


Song Yang1, Jia-Liang Lu1,3, Fenggang Wu1, Linghe Kong1, Wei Shu2 and Min-You Wu1
1Dept. of Computer Science & Engineering, Shanghai JiaoTong University, Shanghai, China
2Dept. of Electrical & Computer Engineering, University of New Mexico, USA
3Universite de Lyon, INSA-Lyon, CITI, INRIA Socrate, F-69621, Villeurbanne, France

Abstract-Recent advances in wireless communication and electronic manufacture have enabled a variety of sensors to be used for Wireless Body Area Networks (WBAN). With these sensors, WBAN can monitor and feedback real-time sensor data for human health monitoring, sports training and interactive environment. But the high dynamic of human movement has been a hard rock on the way of the development of WBAN, which leads to changeful network topology. In this paper, a multi-hop prototype WBAN has been built for characterizing the network connectivity during human motions. A probability based routing protocol fusing inertial sensor data and history link quality is then developed, which aims at taking into count the high spatio-temporal change of WBAN topology on the selection of a reliable relay node in WBAN routing. Compared with a number of existing routing, the proposal protocol can provide better performance in terms of packet delay and delivery ratio. Hence, this kind of routing being aware of human behavior by using inertial sensors is more promising for real-time on body system.
